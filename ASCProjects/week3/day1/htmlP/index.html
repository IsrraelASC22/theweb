The Rhombicosidodecahedron

A Johnson solid with 62 sides, it is basically the fusion of two platonic solids, the dodecahedron & the icosahedron. It is made up of 12 pentagons, 20 triangles, & 30 square faces.

Tidbits of Related Information

Platonic Solid

  • Regular polygons that are identical
  • same number of polygons touching at each vertice
  • convex: the polygons don't cave inwards, only outwards

Only 5 meet the above criteria, those being the Tetrahedron, Cube, Octohedron, Icosahedron, & the Dodecahedron.
